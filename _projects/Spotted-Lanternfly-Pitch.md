## Client Outreach Summary

**Clients:**  
- Cornell CALS Extension  
- E&J Gallo Winery  
- National Grape  

This project explores mechanical methods to remove or deter Spotted Lanternfly (SLF) from grape vines while preventing crop damage and contamination.

---

## Problem Statement

Removing adult SLF from grape plants presents multiple engineering challenges:

- Risk of damaging vines or grapes during removal
- Risk of contamination if SLF are killed directly on the plant
- Chemical pesticides are largely ineffective
- Stronger chemicals may harm grape plants
- Difficulty distinguishing SLF from plant material during removal

A mechanical system must therefore remove SLF without damaging vines or interfering with harvest operations.

---

## Impact and Motivation

A successful mechanical removal or deterrence system could:

- Increase grape yields
- Reduce labor spent on monitoring and spraying
- Lower dependence on ineffective chemical controls
- Improve harvest efficiency
- Reduce long-term economic losses in vineyards

---

## Proposed Mechanical Directions

### 1. Harvester-Mounted Suction Removal System

**Concept:**  
A suction (“vacuum”) device mounted ahead of the grape harvester shaker to remove SLF prior to harvesting.

**Minimum Viable Product Goals:**
- Small-scale suction module
- Remove SLF without pulling leaves or grapes
- Avoid stem damage
- Capture insects into a contained chamber

**Long-Term Vision:**
- Adjustable suction strength
- Integration into existing harvester systems
- Durable, maintainable, field-ready design

**Key Technical Considerations:**
- SLF grip strength varies by life stage
- Required airflow may approach wind speeds of approximately 64–83.8 km/h
- Risk of vacuuming plant material
- Accessibility limitations depending on SLF location
- Added mechanical complexity must not interfere with harvest operations

---

### 2. Decoy-Based Attract-and-Capture Device

**Concept:**  
Attract SLF away from vines using frequency, shape, scent, or light, followed by capture and elimination.

**Minimum Viable Product Goals:**
- Small-scale deployable unit
- One or more lure mechanisms
- Integrated capture system
- Simple removal/elimination process

**Long-Term Vision:**
- Multiple deployable stations across vineyard layouts
- Modular design adaptable to vineyard scale

**Key Technical Considerations:**
- Effectiveness of attractants requires validation
- Risk of attracting other insects or unintended species
- Environmental durability requirements

## Engineering Unknowns

### Suction System Unknowns
- Can existing harvesters accommodate mounting?
- Optimal placement ahead of shaker mechanism?
- Acceptable threshold for vine or fruit damage?
- Required airflow vs. energy consumption tradeoffs
- Seasonal vs. pre-harvest operational needs

### Decoy System Unknowns
- Most effective attractant modality
- Capture efficiency rate
- Maintenance requirements
- Non-target ecological impact

---

## Client Questions Identified

1. Can the harvester be modified to mount a suction device ahead of the shaker?  
2. What level of plant damage (if any) is acceptable to ensure SLF removal?  
3. Is SLF removal required year-round or only during specific growth stages?
